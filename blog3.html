Black fungal disease

BLACK FUNGUS, called as mucormycosis is a fungal infectious disease. Itâ€™s a rare but fatal infection. This infection is presented as fungus growing mostly in and around blood vessels, sinuses, oral cavity, brain or lungs. It can also infect other parts of body too. Patients may test negative for COVID but still have black fungal disease

Why are COVID patients getting black fungal disease?

1. Inappropriate usage of steroids
2. High blood sugar levels (uncontrolled diabetes
3. Low immunity
4. Prolonged stay in ICU
5. Prolonged oxygen therapy
6. Poor maintenance of humidifiers
7. Poor hygienic environment

Black fungus can be any where in soil, wet surfaces, or decaying organic matter.

Symptoms :

RHINOCEREBRAL MUCORMYCOSIS - affecting sinus and Brain

1. Facial swelling
2. Eye pain
3. Head ache
4. Nasal/sinus congestion
5. Fever
6. Black lesions on nasal bridge or upper inside of mouth


PULMONARY MUCORMYCOSIS - affecting lungs

1. Cough
2. Chest pain
3. Breathlessness or shortness of breath
4. Coughing out blood


Cutaneous mucormycosis - affecting skin

1. Blisters and ulcers which eventually turn black
2. Pain
3. Swelling


Gastrointestinal mucormycosis- affecting stomach

1. Abdominal pain
2. Nausea
3. Gastrointestinal bleeding


Diagnosis :

1. Biopsy of infected tissue
2. X-ray
3. CT scan


Treatment :

1. Anti fungal drugs given intravenously or Orally.
2. Surgically removal necrotic/infected tissue
3. Amphotericin B , an anti fungal drug is used
4. Adequate hydration


Who are at risk?

1. Patients with Uncontrolled diabetics mellitus
2. Prolonged and inappropriate Usage of steroids
3. Patients on immunosuppressants
4. Prolonged stay in ICU
5. Prolonged oxygen therapy


Guidelines to prevent mucormycosis/ Black fungal infection in COVID patients :

1. Controlling Blood Sugar .
2. Prescribed administration of steroids in correct dosage and at correct timings with doctor guidelines only .
3. Usage of clean and sterile water for humidifiers during oxygen therapy .
4. Prescribed usage of antibiotics and antifungals only.

This infection is aggressive, severe and fast spreading, it can also be fatal if neglected. Do not hesitate to seek the help of qualified medical professionals.
